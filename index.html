Locked Ground is an acoustic-semiotic construct in which Alexander Chiesa's biographical transit from Italy to Russia becomes sonic matter.

Latin and Russian fragments, including decontextualized
Catholic hymns, form a sacred-industrial
sound field where the artist's name (Chiesa — church)
acquires iconic significance.

Six tracks form a numerological matrix of the cycle:

3 focus on the voice as an autonomous sonic object

3 — its interaction with rhythm

Alternation creates a pulsating perceptual structure — music reduced to ritual machinery.

The title Locked Ground functions as both fixed identity
and potential sonic territory awaiting excavation.

Copycat Department and the disappearing cat point to
simulation, loss of origin, and endless reproduction of meaning.

Created over six months using 12-bit samplers, lo-fi discretization
and digital erosion reconstruct an industrial imaginary between dancefloor and liturgy.
